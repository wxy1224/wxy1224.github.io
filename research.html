Recognition of Paths and Actions in Figure Skating

This is a research project done with Omid Poursaeed (PhD at Cornell Univeresity) and Serge Belongie (Professor at Cornell Tech). This project focuses on applying computer vision techniques on figure skating videos, which include image transformation, motion tracking and human pose recognition.

Some of my tasks include:

  • Apply knowledge in computer vision to calculate skaters’ skating paths

  • Train machine learning models to recognize actions in figure skating videos

  • Facilitate understanding in figure skating programs’ complexity

  • Presented in AOL Connected Experiences Workshop 2016

Calibrating Probabilistic Outputs of Deep Learning Networks

This is a research project done with Kilian Weinberger (Professor at Cornell University). This project aimed to solve the problem that the confidence output from DeepNet does not match its accuracy, and was done with another Cornell undergraduate student Mukund Sudarshan.

Some of my tasks include:

  • Solve the problem that the confidence output from DeepNet does not match its accuracy

  • Apply calibration models to different datasets and compare calibration results
